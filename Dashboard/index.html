⌬⚛🧪🔬🧬🦠 CPP Mechanisms Knowledge Graph

This dashboard explores the Cell-Penetrating Peptide (CPP) Mechanisms Knowledge Graph, a structured RDF representation of the endocytic and non-endocytic pathways through which CPPs enter cells. The graph encodes relationships between mechanisms, genes, inhibitors, cell lines, and cargo types, enabling semantic queries and knowledge discovery across the CPP literature. Source ↗

Data Sources & Ontologies

The CPP Mechanisms Knowledge Graph is built on a combination of community ontologies, biological databases, and a custom CPP schema.

Biological Databases
Ensembl
Gene identifiers (ENSG…) are sourced from Ensembl and resolved via identifiers.org. ensembl.org ↗
ChEBI
Inhibitor compound identifiers (CHEBI_…) drawn from the Chemical Entities of Biological Interest database. ebi.ac.uk/chebi ↗
GO — Gene Ontology
Biological process and cellular component terms used to annotate subcellular delivery locations and uptake mechanisms. geneontology.org ↗
CLO — Cell Line Ontology
Standardised identifiers and hierarchical classification for cell lines used in CPP uptake studies. obofoundry.org ↗
Ontologies
SIO — Semanticscience Integrated Ontology
Core ontology providing entity types and semantic relations used throughout the graph. semanticscience.org ↗
CPP Mechanisms Ontology (custom)
Domain-specific schema defining classes such as CPP-Complex, CellPenetratingPeptide, CargoRole, and UptakeMechanism. w3id.org/cpp ↗
Semantic Web Standards

OWL 2 / RDF / RDFS — The graph is serialised in Turtle (.ttl) and uses OWL 2 constructs together with RDF/RDFS for typing and labelling. W3C OWL ↗ · W3C RDF ↗
